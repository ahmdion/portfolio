Case Study — Corporate Website Redesign

Khatib & Alami

Rebuilding first-impression credibility for one of the region's largest engineering and architecture firms — a strategic redesign of its corporate website (not a corporate portal).

01 — The Challenge

A monumental firm, undersold by its own site.

Khatib & Alami has shaped landmark infrastructure across the region for decades. Its website didn't say so. The existing experience buried that credibility beneath a dated visual language and dense, unstructured pages — forcing visitors to work to understand who the firm was and why it could be trusted.

A heuristic-led audit of the old design surfaced consistent, compounding friction:

  • Buried credibility. Flagship projects and achievements had weak information scent and were several clicks deep — the firm's strongest trust signal was its hardest to find.
  • High cognitive load. Long, flat pages with poor grouping and weak hierarchy made scanning difficult and pushed effort onto the user.
  • Unclear value proposition. On landing, visitors couldn't quickly answer “what do they do, and can I trust them?”
  • Inconsistent navigation. No coherent journey for distinct audiences — prospective clients, partners, and talent all met the same undifferentiated path.
  • Eroded perceived quality. An outdated interface undercut the credibility of a world-class engineering practice.

02 — Strategic Approach

Diagnose with evidence, not opinion.

Before proposing a single layout, I established why the current site underperformed — grounding every later decision in defensible reasoning.

  • Heuristic evaluation. I assessed the experience against Nielsen's ten usability heuristics, isolating breakdowns in visibility of system status, match between system and the real world, consistency, recognition over recall, and aesthetic-and-minimalist design.
  • Competitive & comparative analysis. I benchmarked leading global and regional engineering, architecture, and consulting firms to map category conventions, user expectations, and clear opportunities to differentiate on credibility.
  • Content audit & prioritization. I inventoried existing content and ranked it by what actually drives a decision-maker's trust — then cut, merged, and re-sequenced around it.
  • Design principles. The findings converged on three principles that governed the rest of the project: clarity, credibility, and hierarchy.

03 — Execution

A rebuilt journey and a trust-first visual system.

User journey & information architecture. I restructured the site around a clear narrative — capability, proof, and contact — and surfaced flagship sectors and projects early so credibility is felt within the first screens. Progressive disclosure keeps each page scannable, letting visitors go deeper only when they choose to.

Visual design. I introduced a confident typographic hierarchy, a disciplined grid, and generous whitespace to lower cognitive load and signal quality. Photography of landmark projects became a deliberate trust device rather than decoration, giving the firm's scale immediate, visual proof.

Consistency & accessibility. A componentized system enforced consistency across the site and improved learnability, while WCAG-aligned contrast, structure, and responsive layouts extended the experience cleanly across devices.

04 — Business Impact

Credibility, restored on arrival.

The redesign reframed the firm's decades of work as its primary asset — legible at a glance and impossible to miss. The value proposition now lands in seconds, the user journey guides distinct audiences with intent, and the visual language finally matches the calibre of the practice behind it.

The outcome is a corporate website that does the job a corporate website should: it builds client trust before a conversation ever begins.
